Common Myths

  1. ❌ Coronavirus will go away in Summer months.

    Wrong. Previous pandemics didn’t follow weather patterns plus as we enter summer, there will be winter in the Southern Hemisphere. Virus is global.
  2. ❌ Coronavirus lives in the throat. So drink lots of water so the virus is pushed into the stomach where the acid will kill it.

    Virus may gain entry via throat but it penetrates into the host cells. You can’t wash it away. Excessive water will make you run to the toilet.
  3. ❌ Car accidents kill 30,000 people annually. What’s the big deal with COVID-19?

    Car accident are not contagious, their fatalities don’t double every three days, they don’t cause mass panic or a market crash.
  4. ❌ One of the best strategies to prevent COVID-19 is to clean every door knob in your home with disinfectants.

    Wrong. Hand washing/maintaining 6ft distance is best practice. Unless you’re caring for a COVID patient at home, your home surfaces should not be a big risk.
  5. ❌ I am young and healthy, it doesn't matter for me.

    Even if you're young and healthy still you increase the risk of your death significantly. And also the risk of spreading it to others.

Facts

  1. ✅ It Grows very fast by a factor of 2x 📈.

    The COVID-19 virus has a Basic Reporduction Number or R0 of 2.2 Which means an average person transfers it to 2 others who transfer it to 2 others who transfer it to 2 more and thus 2x.
  2. ✅ Washing your hands with soap literally kills the virus 🧼.

    The virus is made up of stuff surrounded by fat. Using any regular soap (even non-alcoholic) breaks the fat and the tears the virus apart.

    Just be sure to wash your hands properly for 20 seconds so that virus at every corner of your hand is tore apart.
  3. ✅ Avoid Shaking Hands 🤝 .

    Everytime you shake your hand with someone containing the virus in their hand you get the virus in your hands too.

    It takes approx. 8 hand shakes before the virus does not transmit further. So if someone shook hands with someone who shook hand with someone who shook hand with someone... you get the idea... 7 times, then you have a probability of getting infected.
  4. ✅ Social Distancing is the key.

    COVID-19 can also transfer by someone coughing/sneezing at you, maintain a good distance with every person, you don't know who shook hand with who, who shook hand with who... not again... just stay away from Humans.
  5. ✅ Avoid Animals 🐶

    There have been evidences where pets like dogs can contract COVID-19 and spread it to other humans while not getting infected. Although it is not entirely sure if it is true.
